# Landing Page Checklist

## Message

- The headline names the user-visible outcome, not the technology.
- The first screen uses language copied from real user complaints.
- The page focuses on one user, one scenario, one pain.
- The product promise is specific enough to be believed.

## Proof

- Shows what the user gets.
- Includes examples, screenshots, reports, demos, or before/after evidence.
- Explains when the product is not a fit.

## Conversion

- Has one primary CTA.
- CTA is waitlist, preorder, buy now, book, upload, or start.
- No section exists only to explain internal product architecture.
- FAQ answers buying objections, not generic company claims.

## Measurement

- Tracks source, keyword, country, language, CTA click, form submit, payment click.
- Defines a pass/fail threshold before traffic starts.
- Records user replies and objections verbatim.
