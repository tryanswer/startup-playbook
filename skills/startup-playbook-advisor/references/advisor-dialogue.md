# Advisor Dialogue

Use these questions to guide early founder conversations. Ask one question at a time unless the user asks for a full checklist.

## First Intake

Start with the highest missing item:

1. Who is the exact user group?
2. In what specific situation does the pain happen?
3. What is the one painful job they are trying to complete?
4. What do they do today instead?
5. How often does this happen?
6. What does the problem cost them: time, money, anxiety, missed revenue, status, or quality?
7. Where do these users already gather or search?
8. What paid alternatives, services, consultants, templates, courses, or products already exist?
9. What proof do we have that they will pay?
10. What is the smallest manual version that could deliver the result without building software?

## Mode Selection

Use the answer pattern to pick a working mode:

- `idea triage`: the idea is broad, evidence is missing, or the user has not named a segment.
- `evidence plan`: the user has a segment but lacks proof of frequency, channel, or paid intent.
- `landing page plan`: the user can name a pain and audience, and needs a smoke test.
- `MVP scope`: the user has validation evidence and needs to avoid overbuilding.
- `growth plan`: the product exists and needs SEO, ASO, content, community, or sales experiments.

## Question Patterns

### If the idea is broad

Ask:

- Which one group should understand this in one second?
- Which painful situation happens often enough to justify a product?
- What would this user type into Google, Reddit, YouTube, TikTok, App Store, or a marketplace?

### If the user says "everyone"

Narrow:

- Pick one: beginner, professional, creator, operator, parent, student, founder, freelancer, local business, enterprise team.
- Pick one situation: before buying, while doing work, after failure, during planning, during reporting, during compliance, during creative production.
- Pick one result: save time, reduce risk, make money, improve appearance, get approval, learn faster, avoid mistakes.

### If willingness to pay is unclear

Ask:

- What paid workaround do they use now?
- Who controls the budget?
- What would they pay for: diagnosis, setup, result, report, automation, consultation, template, subscription, or done-for-you service?
- Can we charge for a manual audit or concierge version before building?

### If the channel is unclear

Ask:

- Where can we find 20 target users this week?
- Which communities contain raw complaints?
- Which search terms have clear intent?
- Which creators, newsletters, app stores, marketplaces, or forums already aggregate this user group?

### If the user wants to build immediately

Respond with:

- The riskiest unknown.
- The cheapest validation test.
- The smallest build that would be acceptable after that test.
- The stop condition that prevents overbuilding.

## Decision Framing

Use these labels:

- `continue`: strong pain, reachable users, and paid intent are present.
- `pivot`: pain exists but segment, channel, promise, or monetization must change.
- `kill`: pain is weak, infrequent, unreachable, free-only, declining, or already solved with low switching motivation.

Always explain what evidence would change the decision.
